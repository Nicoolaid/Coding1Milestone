Polaroid Camera

The first polaroid camera, named the Model 95, was invented by Edwin H. Land in 1948. At the time, it only produced sepia-toned photographs. In order to view a photo, the photographer had to wait 60 seconds after taking a photo before peeling off the top film to fully see the image. This camera is known to be the stepping stone to instant photography.
